Who this is for. The admin panel is a separate application from the student and instructor portal, at its own address. Only administrator accounts can sign in. Everything in this guide affects real student records, so read section 20 before you begin.

1. How to log in to the admin panel

  1. Go to admin.pageinnovations.com.ng. This is a different address from the student portal — bookmark it separately.
  2. Enter your administrator email address and password.
  3. If two-factor authentication is enabled, enter the current 6-digit code from your authenticator app.
  4. You arrive at the admin dashboard.
Administrator accounts are the most sensitive on the platform. They can read and change every student record. Use a strong, unique password, enable two-factor authentication, never share the login, and always sign out on shared machines. After eight failed attempts an account is temporarily locked for about 15 minutes.

2. How to read the admin dashboard

The dashboard is your daily overview of the platform: how many users and students there are, enrollment and course activity, revenue, and recent events. Use it to spot what needs attention — applications waiting for review, new enrollments, and unusual drops in activity.

Treat it as a starting point rather than a report. For real analysis, use Analytics (section 17).

4. How to manage users

  1. Click Users in the sidebar.
  2. Use the search box to find someone by name or email, and the filters to narrow by role or status.
  3. Click a row to open that person's profile.

Understanding the role badge

The list shows each person's effective role — the role that actually reflects what they can do:

BadgeWhat it means
Super Admin / AdminAdministrator accounts.
InstructorApproved to teach. They may also hold a student capability underneath.
StudentA learner with no teaching approval.

Opening a profile reveals the detail behind the badge. An approved instructor who has also enrolled in a course reads as Instructor (approved) · also a Student (N enrolled). An instructor with no enrollments simply reads as an instructor. Someone whose application is pending shows as a student with an application under review.

Why every account starts as a student. Nobody can sign up as an instructor. Both the registration form and Google sign-in create student accounts, and teaching access is only ever granted through the application and approval process, or through staff onboarding. This is a deliberate safeguard — a person cannot promote themselves.

What you can do from a user profile

  • Review their details, enrollments, and activity.
  • Correct their name or email if it was entered wrongly.
  • Manage their account status.
  • Review their instructor status if they have one.
Deleting a user is not a routine action. Removing an account takes their enrollments, progress, submissions, and grades with it. If someone has simply left the programme, deactivate rather than delete, so the academic record survives.

5. How to onboard a student

The Onboarding Centre is how Page Innovations registers a student properly from the office, with a complete record — the same information a college registry keeps. It creates the account, captures personal, next-of-kin, and academic details, and enrolls the student in their programme in one pass.

  1. Click Onboarding in the sidebar.
  2. Choose Add Student.
  3. Work through the four steps of the wizard, described below. The stepper at the top shows where you are.
  4. Use Next and Back to move between steps.
  5. Check the Review step carefully, then submit.

Step 2 — Next of Kin

The student's emergency contact: full name, relationship, contact number, and residential address. Get the contact number right — this is the number that gets called in an emergency.

Step 4 — Review and submit

  1. Read every row on the review screen. Check the email address character by character — a wrong address means the student never receives their credentials.
  2. Confirm the programme is the one they paid for.
  3. Submit.
  4. The account is created and a temporary password is displayed. Use the copy button next to it.
  5. Give the student their email address and temporary password, and tell them to change it at first login.
The temporary password is shown once. Copy it before you leave the screen. If it is lost, the student must use the "Forgot password" flow on the portal instead.
Which route should a student take? Use the Onboarding Centre when the office is registering someone with their full record — walk-ins, campus intakes, corporate cohorts. Let students self-register on the portal when they can complete their own details; they pick their course during signup and are enrolled automatically.

7. How to approve an instructor

This is where teaching access is granted. It is the platform's main safeguard, so treat it as a genuine review rather than a formality.

  1. Expand the People group and click Instructor Applications.
  2. The page shows counts by status and a list of applications with the applicant's name, status, and submission date.
  3. Click an application row to open the full detail.
  4. Review everything: biography, qualifications, experience, subjects, phone, address, portfolio link, CV, and each uploaded credential document. Open the documents — do not approve on the summary alone.
  5. Decide, using the buttons on the application.

The three decisions

ActionEffectReason required?
Approve Teaching access is switched on for their existing account. They can reach the instructor portal after signing out and back in. In the Users list they now read as Instructor. No
Reject The application is declined and the applicant keeps their ordinary account. Recommended
Revoke Withdraws teaching access from someone previously approved. Yes — required
Revoking is serious. It removes access to courses, grading, and student records. Record a clear reason — the field is compulsory precisely so there is an accountable trail. Check first whether their courses need reassigning to another instructor.

What to check before approving

  • Do the qualifications actually match the subjects they want to teach?
  • Do the uploaded documents open, and do they belong to this applicant?
  • Does the experience stand up — real roles, real durations?
  • Does the portfolio link work?
  • Has Page Innovations verified this person by its normal recruitment process?
Instructor applicants skip email verification by design — your review is the identity check that replaces it. That makes the thoroughness of this review important.

8. How to build and publish a course

Page Innovations curates its catalogue centrally: administrators create courses, and instructors teach the ones assigned to them.

Creating the course

  1. Click Courses in the sidebar.
  2. Click Create course.
  3. Enter the title, description, category, and price, and upload a cover image.
  4. Save it. New courses start as drafts, invisible to students.

Adding modules and lessons

  1. Open the course and go to its builder.
  2. Click Add Module, give it a title — required — and a description, then save. Modules are the major units of the course.
  3. Expand a module and click Add Lesson inside it.
  4. Give the lesson a title and add its content — video, article, or attached file.
  5. Save, and use the preview to check it renders as a student will see it.
  6. Repeat until the outline is complete, then arrange modules and lessons in teaching order.

Publishing

  1. Review the whole outline for gaps, wrong ordering, and broken uploads.
  2. Confirm the category, description, and price are right.
  3. Change the course status to published.
Publishing has a side effect worth knowing. Published courses appear in the course picker on the student registration form. Publishing a half-built course means students can select it and enrol into empty material. Publish only when it is genuinely ready.

9. How to manage categories

  1. Expand the Learning group and click Categories.
  2. Add, rename, or remove categories as needed.

Categories organise the catalogue and help students browse. Keep the list short and meaningful — a handful of broad subjects beats dozens of overlapping ones. Check what a category contains before deleting it, so you do not orphan courses.

10. How to assign an instructor to a course

Instructors cannot assign themselves — this is always an administrator action, and it is what makes a course appear in an instructor's My Courses.

  1. Open Courses and select the course.
  2. Find its instructor setting.
  3. Choose the instructor. Only approved instructors appear in the list — if someone is missing, their application has not been approved yet (see section 7).
  4. Save.
  5. Tell the instructor. The course now appears in their portal with its enrolled students, and they can manage its content, sessions, assignments, and tests.

11. How to manage enrollments

Enrollment is what connects a student to a course. Most enrollments happen automatically — at signup, when the student picks their programme, or through the Onboarding Centre. Use this page for corrections and for cohorts.

Reviewing enrollments

  1. Click Enrollments in the sidebar.
  2. Search and filter to find the records you need. The list shows the student, the course, and the status.

Enrolling one student

  1. Click Enroll student.
  2. Select the student.
  3. Select the course.
  4. Confirm. The student now sees the course in their portal, is added to its chat room, and receives the usual notifications.

Enrolling a whole cohort at once

  1. Click the Bulk enroll option.
  2. Follow the on-screen instructions to supply your list of students and the target course.
  3. Review what the screen reports before confirming — check the number of rows matches what you expected.
  4. Confirm, then spot-check a few students in the list to be sure it worked.
Bulk enrollment is the fastest way to make a big mess. Verify the course and the student list before confirming. Run one small batch first if you are unsure of the format.
Removing an enrollment removes the student's access to that course and their progress within it. Confirm you have the right student and the right course before you unenroll.

12. How to manage the question bank

The question bank is the shared pool instructors draw on when building tests. It is administered centrally at Page Innovations.

  1. Expand the Learning group and click Question Bank.
  2. Browse by category, or search for a specific question.
  3. Add a question, or open an existing one to edit it.
  4. When adding, set the question text, the answer options, the correct answer, the course or category it belongs to, and its difficulty.
  5. Save.

Importing questions in bulk

Questions can be imported from a CSV file rather than typed one at a time. Follow the on-screen format instructions exactly, and import a small test batch first to confirm your file is right before loading hundreds of rows.

Check the answer key. A question with the wrong correct answer marked will penalise every student who answers it properly — and it is usually discovered only after a test has been sat. Review new questions before they go into circulation.
Access is gated by enrollment. Students only ever see questions belonging to courses they are enrolled in, and instructors only those for courses they teach. This is enforced by the platform, not by convention.

13. How to oversee tests and results

  1. Expand the Learning group and click Tests.
  2. Review the tests that exist across the platform, their courses, and their settings.
  3. Open a test's results to see attempts and scores.

Day-to-day test creation and result release belong to instructors. Use this view for oversight — checking that assessment is actually happening, investigating a disputed result, or helping an instructor who is stuck.

If students report missing scores, the test is almost certainly set to withhold results and the instructor has not released them yet. Point the instructor to the release control rather than changing anything yourself.

14. How to review payments

  1. Click Payments in the sidebar.
  2. Review transactions and revenue records, and search for a specific student's payment.
Payment records are the Payments page's business alone. Never try to correct a payment by editing an enrollment, a user, or a course — those actions must not write payment state. If a payment record looks wrong, resolve it here, and escalate anything you cannot explain rather than patching around it.

Where fees are collected at the Page Innovations office rather than online, this page may show little activity. That is expected — the student portal's billing page is switched off for exactly this reason.

15. How to publish an announcement

  1. Click Announcements in the sidebar.
  2. Click Create.
  3. Write a specific title and a clear body.
  4. Choose the audience — the whole platform, or a particular course.
  5. Publish. Recipients see it in their portal and receive a notification.
Reserve platform-wide announcements for things that genuinely affect everyone — closures, system maintenance, policy changes. Course-level notices are the instructor's job. Broadcast too often and people stop reading.

16. How to use the Inbox and moderate chat

Inbox

  1. Expand the Messages group and click Inbox.
  2. Read and reply to direct conversations with instructors and students.

Chat Moderation

  1. Expand the Messages group and click Chat Moderation.
  2. Review course chat rooms and their activity.
  3. Remove inappropriate content where Page Innovations policy requires it.
Moderate with a light touch. Course chat is a teaching space. Intervene for conduct breaches, harassment, or leaked assessment answers — not for ordinary disagreement or off-topic chatter, which the instructor can handle.

17. How to read analytics and activity logs

Analytics

  1. Expand the Reports group and click Analytics.
  2. Review enrollment trends, course performance, completion rates, and revenue.

Useful questions to bring to this page: which programmes fill and which do not; where students stop completing lessons; whether attendance holds up through a cohort.

Activity Logs

  1. Expand the Reports group and click Activity Logs.
  2. Review the record of actions taken on the platform.

This is your audit trail. Consult it when you need to establish who changed what and when — a disputed grade, an unexpected enrollment change, or a revoked access.

18. How to configure platform settings

  1. Open Settings from the sidebar or your account menu.
  2. Review the platform configuration available to you.
  3. Change one thing at a time, save, and verify the effect on the student or instructor portal before moving on.
Settings apply to everyone immediately. There is no preview and no draft. Make changes deliberately, ideally outside teaching hours, and note what you changed in case it needs reverting.

19. How to show and hide features

Several features are deliberately hidden for the Page Innovations academy model. Hiding is centrally controlled rather than done by deleting code, so anything switched off can be switched back on without rebuilding it.

Currently hidden — student portal

Practice Tests, Generate Test, Wishlist, Saved Lessons, Certificates, Leaderboard, Bundles, Billing, Refer & Earn, and Knowledge Base. Billing is off because fees are handled at the office.

Currently hidden — instructor portal

Create Course and Contribute Questions, because the catalogue and question bank are curated centrally.

Currently hidden — admin panel

Leads, Bundles, Coupons, Referrals, Badges, and Email Campaigns.

Currently switched on and specific to Page Innovations

The Onboarding Centre, cohort mode — which asks students to select their paid programme during signup — and the enrollment profile form that collects next-of-kin and academic background.

To change what is visible, ask your technical contact. Each hideable feature has a single named switch, and turning one on or off takes effect across the sidebar, the page itself, and its direct web address together — so a hidden feature cannot be reached by guessing a URL.

20. Security, data protection, and housekeeping

Protecting your own account

  • Use a strong, unique password and change any temporary one immediately.
  • Enable two-factor authentication.
  • Never share an administrator login. If another person needs access, they get their own account — shared logins destroy the audit trail.
  • Sign out properly on shared or public machines.

Protecting student and staff data

The platform holds personal data that carries real obligations: next-of-kin contacts, addresses, dates of birth, bank account numbers, salaries, tax identification numbers, pension and health details, and academic records.

  • Open these records only when you have a genuine reason.
  • Never export, photograph, screenshot, or forward them outside Page Innovations.
  • Never discuss a student's grades, attendance, or personal circumstances with anyone not entitled to know.
  • Work on a machine you control, with a locked screen when you step away.

Before any destructive action

Deleting users, removing enrollments, deleting courses or modules, and revoking instructors all destroy records that people depend on. Before you act:

  1. Confirm you have the right record — check the email address, not just the name.
  2. Ask whether deactivating would achieve the same thing while preserving history.
  3. Consider what the action takes with it — deleting a module deletes its lessons; deleting a user deletes their grades.
  4. Where the action affects a person's standing, record why.

A suggested routine

RhythmWhat to do
DailyClear pending instructor applications; check the Inbox; scan the dashboard for anything unusual.
WeeklyReview new enrollments for errors; confirm every active course has an instructor assigned; skim chat moderation.
Per intakeOnboard the cohort; verify enrollments landed on the right programme; check courses are published and complete.
MonthlyReview analytics and payments; audit administrator accounts and remove any that are no longer needed.

21. Troubleshooting and FAQs

An approved instructor still sees the student view.

Their session is holding the old permissions. Ask them to sign out completely and back in, then use the view switcher in the top bar.

Why does an instructor still show a student side on their profile?

Because one account carries both capabilities by design — it lets teaching staff take courses too. The list badge shows their earned role, Instructor. The profile only mentions a student side when they are actually enrolled in something.

Someone says they registered as an instructor but landed in the student portal.

Working as intended — nobody can sign up as an instructor. They must apply through Become an Instructor, and you approve the application. See section 7.

A student signed up with Google and has no course.

Google sign-up skips the course picker. The portal prompts them to select their programme, and a banner nags until they do. Alternatively, enrol them yourself from the Enrollments page.

A student enrolled in the wrong course.

Fix it on the Enrollments page: remove the wrong enrollment and add the correct one. Students cannot do this themselves.

An instructor cannot see a course they are meant to teach.

They have not been assigned to it. Open the course and set its instructor — see section 10.

A course is not appearing for students.

It is still a draft. Publish it. Remember that publishing also makes it selectable on the signup form.

A student never received their verification email.

Have them check Spam, Junk, and Promotions, and use the resend option. If it still fails, verify the address on their account is correct and correct it if not. Persistent failures across many students point at the email service — escalate to your technical contact.

A student lost their temporary password.

Temporary passwords are shown once and cannot be retrieved. Have them use "Forgot password" on the student portal.

A user is locked out.

Eight consecutive failed logins lock an account for about 15 minutes. Advise them to wait and then reset their password rather than continuing to guess.

Students report missing test scores.

The instructor set the test to withhold results and has not released them. Direct the instructor to the release control.

A student disputes their attendance.

Their instructor owns the register and can correct it from the session's attendance view. If you need to establish what happened, consult the Activity Logs.

Where are Coupons, Bundles, Referrals, and Email Campaigns?

Switched off for the academy model. They can be re-enabled — see section 19.

Bulk enrollment put students in the wrong course.

Remove the incorrect enrollments from the Enrollments page and re-run against the right course. In future, verify the target course before confirming, and trial one small batch first.
