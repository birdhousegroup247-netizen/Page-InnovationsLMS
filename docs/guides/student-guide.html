A note on what you will see. The Page Innovations portal is set up for our academy programme. That means some features you may have seen on other learning platforms — a shop, a wishlist, coupons, or a public leaderboard — are deliberately switched off. Your portal is focused on the course you enrolled in: your lessons, your attendance, your assignments, and your tests. If a menu item named in this guide is missing from your screen, it has been turned off for your programme and you do not need it.

To use the portal comfortably you need four things:

2. How to create your student account

There are two ways a student account comes into existence. Read the one that matches your situation.

Option A — You register yourself (most students)

  1. Open your browser and go to learn.pageinnovations.com.ng.
  2. On the landing page, click Sign Up (or Register). If you land on the login page instead, look for the Create an account link underneath the login form.
  3. Fill in Full Name exactly as it should appear on your records and certificates. This is the name your instructors will see.
  4. Enter your Email Address. Type it carefully — a typo here means you will never receive your verification link.
  5. Create a Password. It must be at least 8 characters and must contain an uppercase letter, a lowercase letter, and a number. For example, a password like Lagos2026 satisfies all three rules.
  6. Re-type the same password in Confirm Password. If the two do not match, the form will tell you before it lets you continue.
  7. Open the "Which course did you enrol in?" dropdown and select the programme you registered and paid for. This step is required. Selecting it here is what automatically places you into the right class.
  8. Optionally expand the extra fields to add your phone number, country, date of birth, or a profile picture. You can always add these later.
  9. Tick the box to agree to the Terms of Service and Privacy Policy. The form will not submit until you do.
  10. Click Create Account.
Choose the right course. The course you pick in step 7 is the class you will be enrolled into. If you select the wrong one, do not create a second account — contact the Page Innovations administrator and ask them to move your enrollment. Creating duplicate accounts with the same email will simply fail, and duplicates with different emails split your progress records.

Option B — The administrator creates your account for you

For some intakes, Page Innovations staff register students directly from the admin office using the Onboarding Centre. If this applies to you:

  1. You will be given (or emailed) your email address and a temporary password.
  2. Go to learn.pageinnovations.com.ng and click Sign In.
  3. Enter the email and the temporary password exactly as supplied — they are case-sensitive.
  4. Once you are inside, change the password immediately. See section 17 of this guide.

Students onboarded this way are usually already enrolled in their programme and already have their profile details on file, so you can skip sections 3, 5, and 6.

3. How to verify your email address

After you create your account yourself, the portal does not log you straight in. It first needs to confirm the email address belongs to you.

  1. After clicking Create Account, you are taken to a "Verify your email" screen showing the address you registered.
  2. Open your email inbox in another tab or on your phone.
  3. Find the message from Page Innovations. The subject line refers to verifying your account.
  4. Click the verification button or link inside that email.
  5. Your browser opens the portal and confirms the address is verified. You can now sign in.

If the email does not arrive

  • Wait two or three minutes. Delivery is usually instant but can lag at busy times.
  • Check your Spam, Junk, and Promotions folders. This is where the message ends up most often. If you find it there, mark it as "Not spam" so future portal mail lands in your inbox.
  • Use the resend option on the verification screen to send a fresh link.
  • Check for a typo in the address you registered. If it is wrong, contact the administrator — they can correct it on your account.

4. How to log in for the first time

  1. Go to learn.pageinnovations.com.ng.
  2. Click Sign In.
  3. Enter your registered email address and your password.
  4. Click Sign In.

If you are asked for a 6-digit code

If two-factor authentication has been switched on for your account, the portal will ask for a one-time code after your password is accepted. Open your authenticator app (or your email, depending on how it was set up), read the current 6-digit code, and type it in. Codes expire quickly, so if it is rejected, wait for the next one to appear and try again.

If you are asked to choose "Student" or "Instructor"

A small number of people at Page Innovations both teach and study. If your account has both capabilities, you will see a chooser screen after logging in. Click the Student tile to enter the learning portal. You can switch between the two at any time using the switcher in the top bar.

Account locking. For your protection, eight consecutive failed login attempts temporarily lock the account for about 15 minutes. If this happens, stop guessing, wait the 15 minutes, and then use Forgot password instead.

5. How to claim your course

Normally you are enrolled automatically because you selected your programme during signup. But if you registered using the Sign in with Google button, that shortcut skips the course picker — so the portal has no way of knowing which class you belong to.

When this happens, the first time you reach your dashboard the portal sends you straight to a "Select your course" screen, and a reminder banner stays on your dashboard until you deal with it.

  1. On the "Select your course" screen, review the list of available programmes.
  2. Click the course you registered and paid for at Page Innovations.
  3. Confirm your choice.
  4. You are enrolled and taken to your dashboard, where your course now appears under My Courses.
Pick carefully. Select the programme you actually paid for. If you choose the wrong one, contact the administrator to have it corrected — you cannot unenroll yourself.

8. How to open a course and study a lesson

  1. Click My Courses in the sidebar.
  2. Click your course card. If you have studied before, the quickest route is the Continue button on your dashboard, which jumps straight to the next unfinished lesson.
  3. The course player opens. The lesson content fills the main area; the course outline sits alongside it, organised into modules (the major sections) with lessons inside each one.
  4. Click any lesson in the outline to open it. Depending on how your instructor built it, a lesson may be a video, a written article, a downloadable file, or a mix.
  5. Work through the content. For video lessons use the player controls to pause, rewind, and adjust the speed and volume.
  6. When you finish, mark the lesson complete (or let it complete automatically when you reach the end of the video). Completed lessons get a tick in the outline.
  7. Move to the next lesson using the Next control or by clicking it in the outline.

Understanding your progress

Your progress percentage is simply the share of the course's lessons you have marked complete. It updates as you go, and it is what your instructor sees when they check how the class is doing. So do mark lessons complete — otherwise your instructor may think you have fallen behind.

Locked lessons. If a lesson will not open, one of two things is happening: the course uses scheduled release, so that material unlocks on a set date; or the lesson has a prerequisite you have not completed yet. Finish the earlier lessons first, and if it still will not open, ask your instructor.

9. How to mark your attendance in a live class

Attendance at Page Innovations is taken with a 6-digit code. Your instructor generates it and displays it during the session — on screen in the classroom, or shared on screen during a virtual class. You enter that code in the portal to record that you were present.

  1. During the live class, click Attendance in the sidebar, under the Learn group.
  2. Find the session in the list. Live sessions appear at the top with a red pulsing dot.
  3. Click Mark attendance on that session. A small window opens.
  4. Read the 6-digit code your instructor is displaying and type it into the box.
  5. Click Check in.
  6. Your status for that session changes to present, and it is saved to your attendance record.

Why the code is time-limited

Each code expires after a short window and your instructor can generate a fresh one at any time. This is deliberate: it ensures attendance reflects who was actually in the room or on the call. Passing your code to an absent classmate is a breach of Page Innovations academic policy.

If the check-in button is not there

  • The session is not live yet. You can only check in while it is running. The page refreshes itself the moment your instructor generates a code, so simply leave it open and the button will appear.
  • You are not enrolled in that course. Only enrolled students can check in.
  • The session has ended. Late check-ins are not possible — speak to your instructor, who can adjust the record.

If your code is rejected

  • Check each digit. A 0 and an O are easy to confuse at a distance.
  • The code may have expired while you were typing. Ask your instructor to display the current one.
  • Make sure you are checking in to the correct session if several are listed.

Reviewing your attendance record

The same Attendance page lists your history for every session, so you can see at a glance which classes you were marked present for. If you believe a record is wrong, raise it with your instructor — only they and the administrator can amend it.

10. How to join a live session

Live sessions are your scheduled classes. Depending on how your instructor set it up, the class runs on Zoom or another meeting platform, and the portal holds the joining link.

  1. Check your dashboard, which lists upcoming sessions, or open your course to see its schedule.
  2. A few minutes before the start time, find the session and click Join.
  3. The meeting opens in a new tab or in your Zoom app. Allow it to use your microphone and camera if you are asked and your instructor expects participation.
  4. Once the session is under way, remember to mark your attendance using the code — see section 9. Joining the call does not automatically record you as present.
Join a few minutes early the first time, so that any microphone, camera, or app installation prompts are sorted out before teaching begins.

11. How to submit an assignment

  1. Click My Assignments in the sidebar, under the Learn group. Your dashboard also flags assignments that are due soon.
  2. Review the list. Each row shows the assignment title, the course, the due date, and its current status — not yet submitted, submitted, or graded.
  3. Click the assignment to open it and read the full brief, the marks available, and the deadline.
  4. Prepare your work. Unless your instructor says otherwise, submit documents as PDF so your formatting survives.
  5. Attach your file, or type your answer into the response box, according to how the assignment was set up.
  6. Check it over — filename correct, right document, nothing missing.
  7. Click Submit. The status changes to submitted and your instructor is notified.

Seeing your grade and feedback

When your instructor has marked your work, the assignment status changes to graded and you receive a notification. Open the assignment to see your score and the written feedback your instructor left. Read the feedback properly — it is the most useful part.

Deadlines are real. Submit before the due date. A late submission is flagged as overdue, and whether it is accepted is entirely at your instructor's discretion. If circumstances are going to make you late, message your instructor before the deadline, not after.

12. How to take an assigned test

Your instructor sets tests and assigns them to the class. They appear under My Assigned Tests.

Before you start

  • Find a quiet place with a stable internet connection.
  • Use a laptop or desktop if you can — small screens make long questions painful.
  • Close other tabs and silence your phone.
  • Check whether the test has a time limit and set aside enough uninterrupted time to finish it.

Taking the test

  1. Click My Assigned Tests in the sidebar.
  2. Find the test and read its details: the number of questions, the time limit, and how many attempts you are allowed.
  3. Click Start when you are genuinely ready. On a timed test, the clock begins immediately and does not pause.
  4. Answer each question. Your answers are saved as you move through the test, and you can navigate between questions to revisit anything you skipped.
  5. Keep an eye on the timer if there is one.
  6. When you are finished, click Submit. If the timer runs out first, the test submits automatically with whatever you have answered.
Do not close the tab mid-test and do not refresh the page unless you have to. On a timed test the clock keeps running whether your window is open or not.

Getting your results

How quickly you see your score depends on how your instructor set the test up:

  • Immediate results — your score appears as soon as you submit, and you can review your answers.
  • Withheld results — you see confirmation that your test was submitted, but no score. Your instructor releases results to the class when marking is complete. You will be notified when yours is available.

If you see no score straight away, nothing has gone wrong — your instructor is simply releasing results later.

13. How to keep notes while you study

  1. Click My Notes in the sidebar, under the Library group.
  2. Click the New note button.
  3. Give the note a clear title — the module or topic name works well.
  4. Type your note.
  5. Save it. Notes are stamped with the date they were created, and they are private to you.

You can return at any time to edit or delete a note. Nobody else — not your instructor, not the administrator — can read them.

14. How to read announcements

Announcements are official notices: timetable changes, exam dates, campus notices, and course-specific messages from your instructor.

  1. Click Announcements in the sidebar, under the Community group.
  2. Announcements are listed newest first. Click one to read it in full.

New announcements also raise a notification, so watch the bell icon. Check this page at the start of each week — schedule changes are posted here.

15. How to message your instructor and classmates

  1. Click Messages in the sidebar, or the message icon in the top bar.
  2. The left panel lists your conversations: your course chat room, shared with your class and instructor, and any one-to-one conversations.
  3. Click a conversation to open it.
  4. Type in the box at the bottom and press Enter to send. Messages arrive in real time — no refreshing needed.

Messaging one person directly

Open the members panel for your course chat to see everyone in your class, and tap a name to start a private conversation with that person or with your instructor.

Understanding the badges

A count on the Messages icon shows your total unread messages. Each conversation shows its own unread count, so you can tell at a glance where the new activity is. Opening a conversation clears its badge.

Keep it professional. Course chat rooms are moderated by Page Innovations administrators, who can review messages and remove anything inappropriate. Treat the chat as part of the classroom.

16. How to manage notifications

The portal notifies you when an assignment is graded, a test is assigned or its results are released, an announcement is posted, a live session is scheduled, and when you receive a message.

  1. Click the bell icon in the top bar for a quick preview of recent alerts.
  2. Click Notifications in the sidebar for the complete history.
  3. Click any notification to jump straight to the item it refers to.
  4. Mark notifications as read — individually or all at once — to clear the badge.

17. How to update your profile, password, and security

Updating your profile

  1. Click your avatar in the top bar and choose Profile.
  2. Update your photo, phone number, and other personal details.
  3. Save your changes.

A real profile photo is worth adding — it helps your instructor and classmates recognise you in chat and in virtual sessions.

Changing your password

  1. Click your avatar and choose Settings.
  2. Find the security or password section.
  3. Enter your current password, then your new password twice.
  4. Save. Remember the rules: at least 8 characters, with an uppercase letter, a lowercase letter, and a number.
If you were given a temporary password by the office, change it the first time you log in. Temporary passwords are handed over in person or by email and should never remain in use.

Resetting a forgotten password

  1. On the login page, click Forgot password.
  2. Enter your registered email address and submit.
  3. Open the reset email and click the link inside.
  4. Choose a new password and confirm it.
  5. Return to the login page and sign in with the new password.

Turning on two-factor authentication

Two-factor authentication adds a 6-digit code on top of your password, so that knowing your password alone is not enough to get into your account. If it is available in your Settings, we recommend enabling it. Follow the on-screen instructions to link your authenticator app, and store any backup codes you are given somewhere safe.

Signing out

Click your avatar and choose Sign Out. Always sign out on shared or public computers — closing the tab is not the same thing.

18. Troubleshooting and FAQs

I registered but never received the verification email.

Check Spam, Junk, and Promotions first — that is where it nearly always is. Use the resend option on the verification screen. If it still does not arrive after several minutes, the address on your account may contain a typo; contact the administrator to correct it.

I signed up with Google and I have no course.

Expected. Google sign-up skips the course picker. The portal will send you to the "Select your course" screen — choose the programme you paid for. See section 5.

My login says "Invalid email or password" but I am sure it is right.

Check that Caps Lock is off, and that you are using the email you registered with rather than another address. After several failed attempts the account locks for about 15 minutes — wait it out, then use Forgot password.

A video will not play.

Refresh the page first. Then try a different browser, and check your internet connection. If you opened the portal from inside a social media app's built-in browser, open it in Chrome or Safari instead. If it fails everywhere, report it to your instructor with the lesson name.

I cannot find the Mark attendance button.

The session must be live and your instructor must have generated a code. The page updates itself when that happens, so leave it open. Also confirm you are enrolled in that course.

My attendance code keeps getting rejected.

Re-read the digits carefully, and remember that codes expire — ask your instructor to display the current one. Confirm you are checking in to the right session.

I submitted my test but I cannot see my score.

Your instructor has chosen to withhold results until marking is complete. You will be notified when they are released.

A lesson is locked.

Either the course releases material on a schedule and that date has not arrived, or you have not completed a prerequisite lesson. Work through the earlier lessons; if it stays locked, ask your instructor.

I enrolled in the wrong course.

Contact the Page Innovations administrator. You cannot change your own enrollment, and you should not create a second account.

There is no Billing page — how do I pay?

Fees are handled directly at the Page Innovations office, not through the portal. That is why Billing is switched off.

Where are my certificates?

Certificates are issued by Page Innovations outside the portal for this programme. Speak to the office about certification for your course.

Can I use the portal on my phone?

Yes — everything adapts to a small screen. But use a laptop for tests and assignments if at all possible.
