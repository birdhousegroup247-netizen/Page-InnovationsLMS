About your portal. Page Innovations runs an academy model: the administration curates the course catalogue centrally, and instructors teach the courses assigned to them. For that reason Create Course and Contribute Questions are switched off in your sidebar. Everything you need to teach — live sessions, attendance, assignments, tests, and student tracking — is fully available.

1. How instructor access works

Understanding this properly prevents most of the confusion new instructors run into.

Nobody signs up as an instructor

Every account created on the portal — whether by the registration form or by Sign in with Google — starts as a student account. There is no "register as instructor" option, and this is by design. Teaching access at Page Innovations is granted, never self-declared. It is earned in one of two ways:

RouteHow it works
You apply You complete the instructor application form. A Page Innovations administrator reviews it and approves or declines. On approval, teaching access is switched on for your existing account.
The office onboards you Page Innovations staff create your account directly through the Onboarding Centre, complete with your employment details, and hand you a temporary password. Teaching access is granted as part of that process.

One account, two capabilities

The portal deliberately keeps a single account per person. When you are approved to teach, your account gains teaching capability but keeps its underlying student base — so if you ever want to take a Page Innovations course yourself, you can, on the same login. You are not "still a student" in any meaningful sense; you simply have both capabilities available. In the admin's user list you appear as an Instructor.

Why Google sign-in is hidden on the instructor login. Because Google sign-up only ever creates a student account, offering it to someone trying to log in as a teacher was misleading. Sign in with your email and password instead.

2. How to apply to teach

Use this if the office has not already created your account.

If you do not yet have an account

  1. Go to learn.pageinnovations.com.ng.
  2. Find the Become an Instructor or Apply to Teach link — it is on the landing page and beneath the login form.
  3. Work through the application wizard. It both creates your account and submits your application in one pass.
  4. Submit.

If you already have a student account

  1. Log in as normal.
  2. On your dashboard, click the Become an Instructor call to action.
  3. Complete the shorter wizard — it reuses the account details you already have.
  4. Submit.

What the application asks for

InformationWhat to provide
Personal detailsFull name, email, phone number, and address.
BiographyA short professional profile. Students may see this, so write it for them.
QualificationsYour degrees, certifications, and professional credentials.
ExperienceYour teaching and industry background — be specific about years and roles.
SubjectsThe subject areas you are competent to teach.
Portfolio linkOptional: a website, GitHub profile, or portfolio.
CV and credential documentsUpload your CV and supporting certificates. The reviewer opens each of these.
Make the reviewer's job easy. A complete, specific application is approved faster than a thin one. Name your uploaded files sensibly and make sure any link you provide actually opens.

3. While your application is under review

When you submit, you land on an "Application under review" page. This is the correct and expected screen — it is not an error.

  • You do not need to verify your email. Instructor applicants skip email verification, because the administrator's review is itself the identity check.
  • Your account exists and works. You can log in, but until approval it behaves as a normal account without teaching tools.
  • Approval is a human decision. A Page Innovations administrator reads your application, opens your documents, and decides. There is no automatic approval and no fixed turnaround — chase the office if it is taking longer than you expected.

The three possible outcomes

OutcomeWhat it means for you
ApprovedTeaching access switches on. Log out and back in, and the instructor portal is available.
RejectedThe administrator records a reason. Contact the office to discuss it or to reapply with stronger detail.
RevokedApplies to a previously approved instructor whose access has been withdrawn, with a reason recorded. Speak to the office.
If you were approved but still see the student dashboard, sign out completely and sign back in. Your session is holding the older permissions until it is refreshed.

4. How to log in and reach the instructor portal

  1. Go to learn.pageinnovations.com.ng.
  2. Click Sign In.
  3. Enter your email address and password. (Google sign-in is intentionally not offered here — see section 1.)
  4. If two-factor authentication is enabled on your account, enter the current 6-digit code from your authenticator.
  5. Because your account can both teach and learn, you are shown a chooser. Click the Instructor tile.
  6. You arrive at your instructor dashboard.
Temporary password? If the office onboarded you and gave you a temporary password, change it immediately after your first login — see section 18.

5. How to switch between teaching and learning

Your account carries both capabilities, and you move between them without logging out.

  1. Look for the view switcher in the top bar — labelled along the lines of Switch to student or Switch to teaching.
  2. Click it. The sidebar and dashboard swap over to the other view.
  3. Switch back the same way whenever you like.

The portal remembers the view you were last in, so you generally land where you left off. If you find yourself looking at the wrong sidebar, this switcher is the fix.

7. How to work with your assigned courses

Courses are created by the administration and assigned to you. You are responsible for the teaching content and delivery within them.

  1. Click My Courses in the sidebar.
  2. You see the courses assigned to you, each with its enrolled-student count and status.
  3. Click a course to open it and manage its content, or to reach its analytics and student list.
A course you expect is missing? Assignment of instructors to courses is done by the administrator. Ask the office to assign you to it — you cannot assign yourself.

8. How to build modules and lessons

Course content is a two-level structure. Modules are the major sections — think of them as weeks or units. Lessons are the individual pieces of teaching inside each module.

Adding a module

  1. Open the course from My Courses and go to its content or builder view.
  2. Click Add Module.
  3. Enter a clear module title — a title is required. "Module 3: Working with Databases" beats "Module 3".
  4. Add a short description explaining what the module covers.
  5. Save. The module appears in the outline, and you can expand or collapse it.

Adding a lesson

  1. Expand the module the lesson belongs to.
  2. Click Add Lesson within that module.
  3. Give the lesson a descriptive title.
  4. Add the content — a video, a written article, or an attached file, depending on the lesson type you choose.
  5. Save. Use the preview to check it renders the way a student will see it.

Editing, reordering, and removing

Modules and lessons can be edited or deleted from the same outline. Order matters — students work top to bottom, so arrange your material in teaching order.

Deleting is not reversible. Removing a module removes the lessons inside it. If you only want to keep material out of students' hands for now, ask the administrator about scheduled release instead of deleting.
Upload sensibly. Compress large videos before uploading, and prefer PDF for handouts so formatting survives on every device.

9. How to schedule and run a live session

Live sessions are your scheduled classes. The portal holds the schedule and the joining link, and notifies your students.

Scheduling a session

  1. Click Live Sessions in the sidebar, under the Teach group.
  2. Click Create (or Schedule session).
  3. Enter a Title — required. Make it recognisable in a list, for example "Week 4 — Query Optimisation".
  4. Add a Description of what the session covers.
  5. Select the Course the session belongs to — required. This determines which students see and are notified about it.
  6. Set the Scheduled date and time — required. Enter it in your own local time; the portal handles the conversion.
  7. Choose the platform:
    • Zoom — the meeting is created automatically when you schedule, and students receive the join link straight away. You do not need to create anything in Zoom yourself.
    • Another platform — paste the Meeting URL yourself. This field is required when you are not using Zoom.
  8. Click Create. The session appears in your list and your students are notified.

Running the session

  1. On the day, open Live Sessions. Sessions are ordered live first, then upcoming, then past.
  2. Click Start / Go Live on the session. Its status changes to live and it shows a red pulsing indicator for students.
  3. Join the meeting itself through the link.
  4. Take attendance — see section 10.
  5. Teach. When you are done, end the meeting in your platform; the session moves into your past list.
Overdue chips. A session past its start time may show an "Overdue" marker. This is only a visual reminder — it never blocks you. You can still start a session late.

10. How to take attendance

Attendance uses a 6-digit code that you generate and display. Students type it into their portal to check in. Codes are deliberately short-lived so that attendance reflects who was genuinely present.

  1. Click Attendance in the sidebar, under the Teach group.
  2. Pick the session you are teaching. Live sessions are listed first.
  3. Click Generate code. A 6-digit code appears with a countdown showing how long it remains valid.
  4. Display the code to your class — project it on screen in the room, or share your screen in a virtual class. You can also copy it to paste into the chat.
  5. Give students a moment to check in. The register updates as they do.
  6. If the countdown expires and students are still checking in, click Generate code again for a fresh one. There is no limit on how many times you can do this.
  7. Review the register before you close the session and correct anything that is wrong.
Generating a code puts the session live. If a session is still marked as scheduled, generating a code flips it to live automatically — otherwise students would have no check-in button. So generating the code is all you need to do.
On code sharing. Short expiry windows exist to stop codes being forwarded to absent students. Generate the code during the class rather than in advance, and regenerate it if you suspect it has been passed around.

Reviewing and correcting the register

The attendance view for each session shows who checked in and who did not. Use it to correct genuine problems — a student whose connection dropped, or someone who arrived after the code expired. Students cannot amend their own records, so they will come to you.

11. How to create and manage assignments

  1. Click Assignments in the sidebar, under the Teach group.
  2. Click Create / New assignment.
  3. Give it a clear title.
  4. Write the brief. Be explicit about what you want, the format you expect, and how it will be marked. Most grading disputes trace back to a vague brief.
  5. Select the course it belongs to.
  6. Set the due date. Enter it in your local time — the portal converts it correctly for everyone.
  7. Set the total marks available.
  8. Save. The assignment appears for your students, and they are notified.

The assignment list shows each assignment with its course, due date, and how many submissions have arrived, so you can see at a glance what is waiting.

12. How to grade assignments

  1. Open Assignments and select the assignment you want to mark.
  2. Open its submissions. Use the filter to show pending submissions — the ones not yet graded — or graded ones you have already dealt with.
  3. Open a student's submission and review their file or written answer.
  4. Enter a score, out of the total marks you set.
  5. Write feedback. This is the part students learn from — say what worked, what did not, and what to do differently next time.
  6. Save the grade. The submission is marked graded and the student is notified.
  7. Move to the next pending submission and repeat.
Grade in batches. Filtering to pending submissions and working straight through is faster and produces more consistent marking than grading one submission whenever it happens to arrive. If you re-open a graded submission, the existing score and feedback are pre-filled, so you can revise a mark cleanly.
Late submissions are flagged as overdue but still reach you. Whether you accept them is your call, in line with Page Innovations policy — apply it consistently across the class.

13. How to create a test

  1. Click My Tests in the sidebar, under the Teach group.
  2. Click Create test.
  3. Enter the test title and select the course.
  4. Set the parameters: the time limit, the number of attempts allowed, and the pass mark.
  5. Decide whether results are shown immediately on submission, or withheld until you release them. See section 14 before choosing.
  6. Add your questions. You can draw on the central question bank for the course, subject to what the administrator has made available.
  7. Save the test, then assign it to your students. They are notified and it appears under their My Assigned Tests.
Take your own test once before assigning it. It is the quickest way to catch a wrong answer key, a confusing question, or a time limit that is far too tight.

14. How to release test results

Tests set to show results immediately need nothing further — students see their score the moment they submit. If you chose to withhold results, you control when the class sees them.

  1. Open My Tests and select the test.
  2. Open its results view, where you can see every attempt and score.
  3. Review the attempts and deal with any marking that needs your attention.
  4. Release results either to the whole class at once, or to individual students if you need to handle someone separately.
  5. Released students are notified and can then see their score and review their attempt.
Why withhold results? It lets you check for problems with a question before the whole class sees a score, and it stops early finishers broadcasting answers to students who have not yet sat the test.

15. How to monitor your students

  1. Click My Students in the sidebar, under the Teach group.
  2. You see the students enrolled in the courses you teach.
  3. Open a student to see their individual progress: lessons completed, assignments submitted and graded, test attempts, and attendance.

Course-level analytics are also available from within a course, giving you completion rates and engagement across the whole class rather than one student at a time.

Reading progress correctly

A student's progress percentage reflects the lessons they have marked complete. A student who watches everything but never ticks lessons off will look inactive. If someone's progress looks implausibly low, ask before assuming they have disengaged.

Check this weekly. A student drifting is far easier to recover in week three than in week nine. Progress plus attendance together give you an early warning.

16. How to post an announcement

  1. Click Announcements in the sidebar.
  2. Click Create / New announcement.
  3. Write a specific title — students scan a list, so "Friday's class moved to 2pm" beats "Important".
  4. Write the body.
  5. Choose the course whose students should receive it.
  6. Post. Students see it under their Announcements and receive a notification.

Use announcements for anything the whole class needs: schedule changes, deadline reminders, material corrections, exam arrangements. Use Messages for individual matters.

17. How to use Messages

  1. Click Messages in the sidebar, or the message icon in the top bar.
  2. The left panel lists your conversations.
  3. Click one to open it, type at the bottom, and press Enter. Messages deliver in real time.

What you will find there

18. How to manage your profile and security

Your profile

  1. Click your avatar in the top bar and choose Profile.
  2. Update your photo, biography, and contact details.
  3. Save.

Keep your biography current — students read it, and it is part of how Page Innovations presents its teaching staff.

Changing your password

  1. Click your avatar and choose Settings.
  2. Open the security or password section.
  3. Enter your current password, then the new one twice.
  4. Save. Passwords need at least 8 characters with an uppercase letter, a lowercase letter, and a number.
Change any temporary password immediately. If the office issued you one during onboarding, replace it the first time you log in.

Two-factor authentication

Instructor accounts can affect student grades and records, so two-factor authentication is strongly recommended. Enable it in Settings, link your authenticator app, and store any backup codes somewhere safe.

Signing out

Click your avatar and choose Sign Out. Always sign out on classroom or shared machines — leaving a session open leaves your grading tools exposed.

19. Good practice and classroom policy

At the start of a course

  • Check your course outline is complete and in teaching order before day one.
  • Post a welcome announcement setting out how you will run the course, your expectations, and how quickly you respond to messages.
  • Confirm your live sessions are scheduled with the correct dates and working join links.

Every week

  • Generate attendance codes during class, not before.
  • Grade submissions promptly — students act on feedback while the topic is fresh.
  • Review progress and attendance to catch students slipping.
  • Answer course chat questions, or say when you will.

Consistency and fairness

  • Apply your late-submission policy the same way for everyone.
  • Say in the brief how work will be marked, before it is submitted.
  • Handle individual matters — grades, conduct, personal circumstances — by direct message, never in the course room.
  • Treat student records as confidential. Grades, attendance, and next-of-kin details are not to be discussed outside Page Innovations.

20. Troubleshooting and FAQs

I was approved but I still land on the student dashboard.

Sign out completely and sign back in — your session is holding the old permissions. After that, use the view switcher in the top bar to move to the teaching view.

Why is there no "Sign in with Google" on my login?

Deliberate. Google sign-in only ever creates a student account, so offering it to teachers was misleading. Use your email and password.

Why can I not create a course?

Page Innovations curates its catalogue centrally, so Create Course is switched off. Ask the administrator to create the course and assign you to it.

A course I am supposed to teach is not in My Courses.

The administrator assigns instructors to courses. Ask the office to assign you.

My students say they cannot see the check-in button.

They can only check in while the session is live and a code exists. Generate a code — that also flips the session live. Their page updates automatically, so they do not need to refresh.

A student missed the code before it expired.

Just generate a new one — there is no limit. If the class has already ended, correct their record from the session's attendance view.

A student says they submitted but I see nothing.

Check you are looking at the right assignment and that your filter is not set to "graded". If it is genuinely missing, ask them to resubmit and report it to the administrator.

My students cannot see their test scores.

The test is set to withhold results. Open the test's results view and release them — to the whole class or to individuals. See section 14.

My live session time looks wrong.

Enter times in your own local time; the portal converts them. If a time still looks off, re-open the session and confirm what is saved, then report it to the administrator.

Zoom did not create a meeting.

Zoom meetings are created automatically when you schedule with the Zoom option. If no link appears, report it to the administrator — the integration is configured centrally. In the meantime, create the meeting yourself and paste the URL into the session.

A student's progress shows 0% but they attend every class.

Progress counts lessons marked complete. Remind your class to mark lessons complete as they finish them.
